About

I am interested in different topics of pure and applied mathematics, thereby touching the broad fields of analysis, ergodic theory and dynamical systems.

Beforehand, I was a postdoc at the Institute for Analysis and Numerics of the University of Münster supervised by Prof. Dr. Marlies Pirner.

I obtained my PhD (Dr. rer. nat.) in Mathematics at the University of Bremen under the supervison of Prof. Dr. Jens Rademacher and Prof. Dr. Marc Keßeböhmer.

Most recently, I worked as a lecturer and research assistant at Hochschule Bremen.

Further details and additional information can be found in my CV.

Research interests

  • Ordinary and partial differential equations and their dynamics
  • Dynamical Systems and Ergodic Theory
  • Kinetic Theory and Entropy methods
  • Boltzmann equation and its BGK-type approximations
  • Long-time behavior of solutions (Hypocoercivity)
  • Models of chemical reactions
  • Physical applications
